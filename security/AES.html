Advanced Encryption Standard

This Encryption standard was established in 2001 by the U.S. National Institute of Standards and Technology, AES was adopted by the U.S. government and is used all over the web, AES uses a four by four matrix of bytes. Typically I have used this type of encryption to send larger chucks of data across the wire, when using RSA at the end points, this is because AES allows for sending larger chunks of data, so that we can send encrypted json data for instance.In 2009 the only successful attacks against AES were side channel attacks, the National Security Agency(NSA) reviewed all the AES variants and concluded that AES encryption is secure enough for all U.S. non-classified data.
